# Insurance Claims Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes healthcare insurance claims data to evaluate claim volume, billed amounts, payer mix, denial reasons, approval rates, and procedure-level revenue performance.

## Objectives

- Analyze insurance claim volume
- Evaluate billed amount trends
- Identify payer mix distribution
- Analyze paid, denied, and under review claims
- Identify top denial reasons
- Calculate claim approval rate
- Identify high-volume diagnosis codes
- Identify procedure codes generating the highest billed amounts

## Tools Used

- SQLite
- SQL
- Tableau

## Key Business Questions

1. How many claims are in the dataset?
2. What is the total billed amount?
3. What is the average claim amount?
4. Which claims have the highest billed amounts?
5. Which providers submit the most claims?
6. How many claims come from each insurance type?
7. How many claims are Paid, Denied, or Under Review?
8. What are the most common denial reasons?
9. What is the claim approval rate?
10. Which diagnosis codes appear most often?
11. Which procedure codes generate the highest billed amounts?

## Key Findings

- The dataset contains 1,000 insurance claims.
- Total billed amount was $297,191.
- Average billed amount per claim was approximately $297.
- The claim approval rate was 33.4%.
- Denied claims were nearly equal to paid claims, suggesting opportunities for revenue cycle improvement.
- Incorrect billing information was the leading denial reason, accounting for about 15% of denied claims.
- Commercial and Medicaid had the highest claim volume among insurance types.
- Procedure Code 99231 generated the highest billed amount, representing approximately 11.7% of total billed charges.
